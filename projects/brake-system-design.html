Vehicle Components

Brake System Design

This brake system design project was done to practically understand the theory, by using brake force distribution and adhesion utilisation diagrams.

Back to Projects

Overview

This project applied vehicle component theory to design a brake system for a two-axle truck. The work focused on predictable brake-force distribution, vehicle stability, and braking behaviour across loaded and unloaded conditions.

Challenges

Balancing loaded and unloaded braking

Challenge: A truck brake system must remain stable when axle loads change significantly between operating conditions.

Solution: Compared loaded and unloaded adhesion utilisation, then selected a brake-force distribution approach that kept rear-axle demand within a safer range.

Summary

The study combined theoretical calculations with MATLAB-supported evaluation to compare ideal brake-force distribution, select a suitable pneumatic brake cylinder configuration, and review adhesion utilisation systematically. The study was done for both loaded and unloaded conditions and emphasized the critical role of proper brake distribution in maintaining vehicle stability and regulatory requirements. The final design approach highlighted the importance of load-sensitive rear axle pressure control and the safety impact of front-brake malfunction. This same work can be expanded to analyse the effect of advanced electronic brake systems or optimizing designs for different vehicle configurations to further enhance safety and performance.

Tools MATLAB, vehicle dynamics calculations
Focus Brake distribution, adhesion utilisation, stability
Scope Two-axle truck, loaded and unloaded cases
