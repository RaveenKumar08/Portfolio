Powertrain Performance

Powertrain Analysis

Experimental and analytical study of vehicle powertrain behaviour using vehicle's track test data.

Back to Projects

Overview

This project analysed the powertrain performance of a Volvo S90 D5 AWD Geartronic using track test data. The test was done for coast-down and acceleration manoeuvres, with the goal of understanding the vehicle's powertrain behaviour and performance characteristics. The analysis involved filtering and processing the test data, applying theoretical models to estimate powertrain parameters, and evaluating the results in the context of vehicle dynamics and efficiency. The work connected vehicle dynamics, transmission behaviour, and energy efficiency into a compact powertrain performance study.

Challenges

Noisy track-test data

Challenge: Coast-down and acceleration runs include measurement noise, changing road-load effects, and transient vehicle behaviour.

Solution: Processed the test data separately for each manoeuvre, filtered the useful operating range, and compared the extracted values with vehicle dynamics theory before interpreting the results.

Summary

The project involved conducting two coast down tests at different speeds and two acceleration tests in second and third gear. For coast-down tests, the vehicle was accelerated to a certain speed and shifted into neutral mode to disconnect the engine from the powertrain, which was used to calculate the rolling resistance coefficient and drag coefficient. For acceleration tests, the vehicle was kept in a quasi-static driving condition of 2500 rpm, and then full throttle was applied until the vehicle shifted automatically into the next higher gear. Another acceleration test was done, where the vehicle was started from a standstill and accelerated by applying full throttle and ending after the vehicle shifted into fourth gear. The acceleration test data was used to derive the maximum engine power for different gears. It also considered how CVT optimisation and hybridisation can influence efficiency, fuel use, and acceleration potential.
